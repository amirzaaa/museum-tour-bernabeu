Santiago Bernabéu Journey

This is a short story about Santiago Bernabéu Journey

  1. Early Construction and Opening (1947-1950)
    • Planning: During his tenure as president of Real Madrid from 1943 to 1978, Santiago Bernabéu maintained a strict attitude when it came to stadium renovations and maintenance. He wanted a stadium that could accommodate more people and improve the club's facilities.
    • Construction: Construction began in 1944 and the stadium was officially opened on December 14, 1947 under the initial name “Estadio Chamartín,” which was later renamed “Santiago Bernabéu” in 1955 in honor of the club president.
  2. Renovation and Expansion (1950-1990)
    • First expansion: In 1953, the stadium was expanded from its original capacity of 75,000 to 125,000 seats. This addition made it one of the largest stadiums in the world at the time
    • Renovation and Modernization: Over the next decade, the stadium underwent several major renovations. In 1982, the stadium became one of the host venues of the 1982 FIFA World Cup and underwent major renovations to add modern conveniences and facilities
  3. Modern Era (1990-recently)
    • Renovation 2000s: In the early 2000s, the stadium underwent major renovations to update facilities and improve spectator comfort. The capacity was reduced to around 81,044 seats, but the stadium gained new features including more comfortable seats and VIP facilities.
    • Recent Renovation Project: In 2019, a major renovation project began with plans to increase the stadium's capacity, update the exterior with an LED-coated facade, and add modern amenities including retail and restaurant areas. The project is expected to be completed by 2024.
  4. Rewards and Influence
    • Awards: The stadium is known for its modern facilities and has hosted numerous international matches, including UEFA Champions League finals, World Cup matches, and European Cup finals.
    • Cultural Influences: The Santiago Bernabéu is a symbol of Real Madrid's prestige and glory and is a top destination for soccer fans from all over the world.
